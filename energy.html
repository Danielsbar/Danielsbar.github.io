Fossil Fuel and Alternative Energy Costs

.

For a long time I have heard people debate on what type of energy is best. Although each of these have their own benefits and problems, such as how much heat they produce, and waste products. People seem most concerned over money.

Since I was a kid, I've heard people talk about solar energy, how 'after a while it will make you money', so I thought it would be interesting to see how that stacks to today and how it compares to other sources.

The values are all based on each producing 1 MWh and adjusted for inflation based on the first year that was evaluated by the Energy Information Administration, 2017.

https://www.eia.gov/outlooks/aeo/assumptions/pdf/elec_cost_perf.pdf

All of the values are based on the initial cost to build and start these energy generation devices and the annual cost to maintain and service them. This is all based on their costs per 1 MWh to try to keep the information as fair for these purposes as possible.

Combustion Turbine with Industrial Frame is the cheapest intial options to build initial at $867,000 per MWh. With the much lower cost to maintain.with the Solar Photovoltaic (PV) with Tracking. After about 16 years, the Solar (PV) with Tracking would be the best option economically. Over about that same time, the Combustion Turbine with Industrial Frame would also become more expensive than Combined-cycle Multi-shaft generator.

The biggest thing holding Solar and other alternative energy sources back from being the more affordable option in a lot of cases is the initial cost being highly than some fossil fuel counterparts. If there were some government incentives to reduce the initial cost burden on the initial investment, it should massively reduce the depend on burning fuel when it is more expensive than the green alternatives.
