Outdoor Recreation vs. Natural Resource Extraction

Outdoor Recreation vs. Natural Resource Extraction

When thinking of ways to make money from land, most commonly people think out building on it, or extracting resources from it, but it turns out that there has been a large economic impact made from recreational use of the natural land and National Parks in the US.

In the Totals, I decided to eliminate supporting recreational activity and the economic impact from apparel and long distance travel. These sources of the economic impact very likely include sales and spending on items and events that are not directly related to land usage.

The numbers are all in millions of dollars and adjusted for inflation based on the first year that was evaluated by the Bureau of Economic Analysis, 2017.

https://www.bea.gov/sites/default/files/2023-11/orsa1123.pdf

Over the last few years, we've seen the economic impact of outdoors recreational activity surpass that which the US was experiencing from using land for extracting resources and manufacturing with those resources, given if all of these recreational activities are sustainable, these shows a great economic benefit in support programs and incentives to expend National Parks and increase resources to increase activity further.

If one was interested in furthering this information for business investment or government policy, it would be helpful to research what kind of growth could be expected from any changes or if the current rates of involvement are at their peak. Studying how the impact changes over the next few years could be helpful to determine what kind of ptential for growth exists.

Given more data, it would be helpful to be able to evaluate all sources of economic impact from only those that concern land usage without having to disregard data that may incorrectly skew it.
