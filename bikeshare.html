Bike Share - Weather

Bike Share - Weather

Please highlight a box over the data you would like to see in more detail on the line graph.

The City of London, England began a bike sharing program in 2010 in order to try to increase cycling and decrease dependency on cars in the city. They expanded this program in 2015 and monitored the bike usage throughout the next 2 years. They evaluated how weather, time of day, day of the year, wind speeds, and temperature affected the bike usage.

You may alter the period of days, weeks or months hich the data is averaged over, and if you hover over any of the data points, you can see the breakdown of weather and hours of greatest use during that period.

https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/hmavrodiev/london-bike-sharing-dataset

One might expect that the periods with the greatest users would be more mild temperatures, without strong winds, and when there isn't inclement weather. The hours that have the highest usage also indicate that most of the riders would use the bikes for their work commutes; 8am, 5pm and 6pm consistently have the most users.

While evaluating the economic impact of Natural Land Usage I found it remarkable how much money is made from biking in this country. From how successful the bike sharing program was in London and the increasing trend in outdoor recreation, it seems that increasing access to bikes for recreation and commuting would have large positive economic impact and reduce traffic in cities.

Given a larger dataset continuing until more recently, it would be more helpful to learn how the trends have persisted. With the increase in remote work since the 2020 pandemic, it is possible that there has been a large reduction since then, but it also possible that instead of decreasing the spread over hours may be a bit more spread and less concentrated around the start and end of work hours.
